A Craft's Humble Beginnings

The art of crochet first emerged around the mid-1820s, quickly gaining significant popularity, particularly in England. Despite its widespread adoption, the precise origins of crochet remain a mystery, with its beginnings claimed by various cultures.

The Essence of the Craft

Fundamentally, crochet developed as a distinct craft throughout the 19th century. It is characterized by the use of a single hook to create interlinked loops of thread, which are then meticulously transformed into intricate works of art using yarn.

The Name Takes Hold

The term "crochet" itself wasn't formally coined in England until 1837. Its earliest known appearance was in a publication titled 'The Ladies' Knitting and Netting Book'.

A French Connection

While primarily an English-language publication, 'The Ladies' Knitting and Netting Book' notably featured one crochet pattern written in French. The term "crochet" was used only once within its pages, directly stemming from the French word for 'hook,' which is the tool central to this unique textile art.

Why crochet?

Crochet is a very relaxing activity. While it may take some time to get the knack of it, it is worth taking the time to learn. There is great satisfaction of turning a normal skein of yarn into anything from a snuggly children's plushie to a soft blanket to warm winter beanie. It is a hobby that is worth giving a try!
